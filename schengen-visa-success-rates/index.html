What Affects Success Rates?

1. Application Volume

High-volume countries (France, Spain, Italy):

  • Lower approval rates (82-83%)
  • Reason: More applicants = more varied profiles = stricter screening
  • Benefit: More established processes, clearer guidelines

Low-volume countries (Baltic states, Slovakia):

  • Higher approval rates (95-97%)
  • Reason: Fewer applications = self-selection (only serious travelers apply)
  • Challenge: Less information available online

2. Tourism vs. Business Focus

Tourism-focused countries:

  • Greece, Portugal, Iceland: Encourage tourism, streamlined for visitors
  • Higher approval rates for genuine tourists

Business hub countries:

  • Switzerland, Belgium, Germany: More scrutiny due to work/migration concerns
  • Lower approval rates, stricter financial checks

3. Geographic Location & Migration Patterns

Border countries:

  • Countries bordering non-EU regions face more irregular migration attempts
  • Greece (Turkey proximity), Croatia, Bulgaria: Extra scrutiny

Northern/Baltic countries:

  • Less migration pressure, established tourism
  • Finland, Estonia, Lithuania: Higher approval rates

4. Financial Requirements

Countries with high daily amounts:

  • Italy (€120/day), Spain (€100/day), Switzerland: Stricter financial scrutiny
  • Natural filter—weak applications self-eliminate

Countries with moderate requirements:

  • Estonia (€30/day), Greece (€50/day): More accessible
  • Broader applicant pool accepted

5. Seasonal Variations

Season Approval Impact Details
Peak Summer (Jun-Aug) Slightly Lower High volume, rushed processing, more fraud attempts
Winter Holidays (Dec-Jan) Moderate Christmas markets, skiing—legitimate tourism
Spring/Fall (Apr-May, Sep-Oct) Higher Lower volume, more careful review, serious travelers
Off-season (Nov, Feb-Mar) Variable More scrutiny on travel purpose (why now?)

How to Interpret These Statistics

What Success Rates DON'T Tell You

❌ Myth: "97% approval = almost guaranteed visa"

✅ Reality: Those 3% rejections are applicants with real issues. If you have weak ties or insufficient funds, you'll be in that 3%!

  • Self-selection bias: Fewer people apply to Lithuania (high rate) vs. France (lower rate), so Lithuania sees only serious applicants.
  • Aggregate data: Rates combine all visa types (tourism, business, family), all nationalities, all circumstances.
  • No personal context: Your individual profile matters infinitely more than country averages.

What Success Rates DO Tell You

  • ✅ Relative strictness: Malta (80%) is genuinely stricter than Lithuania (97%)
  • ✅ Process efficiency: High-approval countries often have clearer, faster processes
  • ✅ Application experience: Lower rates = expect more questions, longer processing
  • ✅ Risk tolerance: High-rate countries may approve borderline cases that low-rate countries reject

The "Main Destination Rule" Overrides Everything

⚠️ Critical: You MUST apply to the country where you'll spend the most time, regardless of approval rates. Applying to Lithuania just for the 97% rate when you're really visiting France is visa fraud!

Correct approach:

  1. Plan your genuine itinerary first
  2. Identify main destination (most nights)
  3. Apply to that country, regardless of approval rate
  4. Strengthen your application to meet that country's standards

Strategic Application Tips

If Your Main Destination Has Lower Approval Rate

Applying to France (83%), Spain (82%), or Italy (82%)?

Compensate with:

  • ✅ Higher financial buffer: 30% above minimum (€1,200 instead of €910)
  • ✅ Detailed itinerary: Day-by-day plans, specific addresses
  • ✅ Stronger proof of ties: Multiple documents showing home country commitments
  • ✅ Updated travel insurance: €50,000 coverage instead of minimum €30,000
  • ✅ Professional cover letter: Address any potential concerns proactively
  • ✅ Early application: Submit 4-6 weeks before travel, not last minute

If You Have Flexibility in Planning

If you genuinely want to visit multiple countries and haven't decided main destination:

Consider favoring:

  1. Greece (88%): If Mediterranean appeals—islands, history, great food
  2. Czech Republic (94%): If Central Europe interests you—Prague, culture
  3. Estonia (97%): If Baltic/Northern Europe—Tallinn old town, digital innovation

Example itinerary adjustment:

  • ❌ Before: 5 nights Paris + 3 nights Athens → Apply to France (83% rate)
  • ✅ After: 3 nights Paris + 5 nights Athens → Apply to Greece (88% rate)

This is legitimate IF Athens was always in your plans!

Common Myths About Success Rates

Myth 1: "I should always apply to the country with highest approval rate"

Truth: You MUST apply to your main destination. Applying elsewhere is fraud. If caught, you'll be rejected and potentially banned.

Myth 2: "High rejection rate means impossible to get visa"

Truth: Even Malta (80% approval = 20% rejection) approves 4 out of 5 applications. Strong applications succeed anywhere.

Myth 3: "My nationality determines everything"

Truth: While nationality affects baseline scrutiny, your individual profile (finances, ties, purpose) matters far more. Strong Indian applicants get approved; weak Japanese applicants get rejected.

Myth 4: "Baltic countries are 'easier' because they want tourism"

Truth: Their high rates reflect self-selection (fewer people apply) and established processes, not leniency. They still enforce all standard requirements.

Myth 5: "Approval rates tell me my chances"

Truth: Rates are aggregates across all applicants. Your personal chance is 99% (strong application) or 5% (weak application), not the country average.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which Schengen country is easiest to get a visa from?

Lithuania (97.2%), Estonia (96.8%), and Finland (95.6%) have the highest approval rates. However, you must apply to your actual main destination, not just the "easiest" country!

Why does France have a lower approval rate than Estonia?

France receives 10x more applications (2+ million vs. 200,000), including many speculative or weak applications due to Paris's popularity. Estonia's high rate reflects fewer, more serious applicants (self-selection).

Do approval rates vary by month or season?

Yes, slightly. Peak summer (June-August) sees marginally lower approval rates due to high volume and more rushed processing. Spring/fall applications often receive more careful review.

If I'm rejected by one country, can I apply to another?

Yes, but it won't help if the rejection reason applies universally (insufficient funds, weak ties). You must address the rejection reason before reapplying anywhere. Also, your rejection is visible to all Schengen consulates.

Are these rates for tourist visas specifically?

The overall rates combine all short-stay visa types (tourism, business, family visit, cultural, sports). Tourist visa approval rates are typically 3-5 percentage points higher than aggregate rates.

Does applying through VFS vs. directly at consulate affect approval?

No. VFS is only an application collection center. All decisions are made by consular officers using the same criteria, regardless of submission method.

Do Indians have lower approval rates?

Indian applicants average 84.6% approval across all Schengen countries, which is below the 86.7% overall average but still means 5 out of 6 applications succeed. Strong applications from India are routinely approved.
