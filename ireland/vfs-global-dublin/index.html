VFS Global Dublin: Complete Guide to Schengen Visa Application Center

Updated: February 1, 2026 · 8 min read

VFS Global Dublin is the main visa application center for multiple Schengen countries in Ireland. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know: location, appointment booking, hours, what to bring, and what to expect during your visit.

⚠️ Always Verify with Official Sources:

While we keep this guide updated, embassy policies and VFS procedures can change. Always check the official VFS Global website and your destination country's embassy website for the most current information, fees, and requirements before your appointment.

How to Book an Appointment

  1. Visit VFS Website: Go to www.vfsglobal.com and select your destination country
  2. Create Account: Register with your email and create a password
  3. Fill Application Form: Complete the online visa application form
  4. Choose Appointment: Select available date and time slot
  5. Pay Appointment Fee: Pay the VFS service fee (separate from visa fee)
  6. Receive Confirmation: Get appointment confirmation email with reference number
⚠️ Important: Appointments fill up quickly, especially during summer and holiday seasons. Book at least 2-3 weeks in advance. Tuesday-Thursday mornings typically have more availability than Mondays or Fridays.

What to Bring to Your Appointment

Essential Documents

  • ✅ Printed appointment confirmation
  • ✅ Original passport (valid 3+ months)
  • ✅ Old passports (if any)
  • ✅ IRP card (Irish Residence Permit)
  • ✅ All required documents (originals + photocopies)
  • ✅ Visa fee payment (€80 adults, €40 children 6-12)
  • ✅ VFS service fee payment

Payment Methods Accepted

⚠️ IMPORTANT - Payment Policy 2026:

VFS Global Dublin and embassies DO NOT ACCEPT CASH PAYMENTS for visa fees. Only card payments are accepted.

Accepted Payment Methods:

  • 💳 Debit card (Visa, Mastercard, Maestro)
  • 💳 Credit card (Visa, Mastercard)
  • ❌ Cash NOT accepted - Card payment mandatory for visa fees

What You'll Pay:

  • Visa Fee: €80 (adults) / €40 (children 6-12) - Card only, paid to embassy
  • VFS Service Fee: €25-€30 - Card only
  • Optional Services: Courier, SMS tracking - Card payment
💡 Pro Tip: Bring a valid debit or credit card in your name. Ensure your card has sufficient funds for both visa fee (€80) and VFS service charges (€25-€30). Some applicants' cards have been declined due to insufficient international payment limits - check with your bank beforehand.

Items NOT Allowed Inside VFS

For security reasons, the following items are prohibited:

  • ❌ Large bags, backpacks, or suitcases
  • ❌ Electronic devices (tablets, laptops) - only phones allowed
  • ❌ Food and beverages
  • ❌ Sharp objects (scissors, nail clippers)
  • ❌ Lighters or matches
  • ✅ Small handbag or document folder (acceptable)
💡 Pro Tip: Bring both originals and photocopies of all documents. VFS staff will verify originals and keep the copies. Some applicants bring extra photocopies just in case.

Tips for Families with Children

If you're applying as a family, here's what to expect:

  • Children Under 12: No biometrics required, but they must attend in person
  • Waiting Area: Limited seating - consider bringing one parent while other waits outside with young children
  • Processing Time: Family applications take longer - allocate 90-120 minutes
  • Documents: Each family member needs complete document set, even children
  • Quiet Environment: VFS is a professional setting - bring quiet activities for children if needed
💡 Family Tip: Book morning appointments (9-10 AM) when children are fresh. Bring snacks and water for after your appointment, as you cannot bring food inside VFS.

What to Expect During Your Visit

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Security Check (5 minutes):

    Enter building, go through security screening. No large bags allowed inside. Small handbags and document folders are fine.

  2. Check-In (5-10 minutes):

    Show appointment confirmation at reception. Receive token number and wait in designated area.

  3. Document Review (15-20 minutes):

    VFS staff checks all your documents for completeness. They'll point out any missing items or issues.

  4. Biometrics Collection (5 minutes):

    Fingerprints scanned digitally. Photo taken (if required). This is quick and painless.

  5. Payment (5 minutes):

    Pay visa fee and VFS service charges at payment counter.

  6. Receipt & Tracking (2 minutes):

    Receive payment receipt and tracking number. Keep this safe - you'll need it to track your application.

Total Time: Expect to spend 45-90 minutes at VFS, depending on crowd levels.

⚠️ Waiting Times: Mornings (9-11 AM) tend to be busiest. Late morning (11 AM-12 PM) or early afternoon slots usually have shorter waits. Fridays are the quietest day of the week.

Biometrics Requirements

All first-time Schengen visa applicants must provide biometrics (fingerprints and photo). This data is stored in the VIS (Visa Information System) for 5 years.

Who Is Exempt?

  • Children under 12 years old
  • Applicants who provided biometrics in the last 5 years (bring previous visa)
  • People physically unable to provide fingerprints

Biometric Process

The process is completely digital and takes about 5 minutes:

  1. Both hands' fingerprints scanned
  2. Digital photo taken (if your photo doesn't meet standards)
  3. Data uploaded to VIS system

Frequently Asked Questions - VFS Dublin

Can I walk in without an appointment?

No, VFS Dublin operates by appointment only. You must book online in advance through the VFS Global website for your destination country. Walk-ins are not accepted under any circumstances.

Does VFS Global accept cash payments?

No, cash is NOT accepted at VFS Dublin. As of 2026, all payments including visa fees and VFS service charges must be made by debit or credit card only. This is a strict policy applied by both VFS and all embassies. Make sure your card has sufficient funds and international payment enabled.

What if I miss my appointment?

You'll need to rebook a new appointment online. Most embassy fees are non-refundable, but you can use the same application for a new slot. Try to reschedule at least 24 hours in advance if possible. Late arrivals (more than 15 minutes) may also be turned away.

Can someone else submit my application for me?

No, you must attend in person for biometrics (fingerprints and photo). Exceptions are only made for children under 12, applicants with previous biometrics (within 5 years), or those with medical conditions preventing attendance (requires medical certificate).

Is VFS Global the embassy?

No, VFS is an external visa application center that collects documents on behalf of embassies. Your application is processed by the actual embassy/consulate. VFS just handles document collection and return. They cannot make visa decisions or provide embassy-specific guidance.

What if VFS says I'm missing a document?

VFS staff will tell you if anything is missing. You can either submit what you have (and risk rejection) or cancel and rebook after getting the missing document. For non-critical documents, submitting with an explanation letter sometimes works. Critical documents like passport, IRP, or bank statements cannot be skipped.

How early should I arrive for my appointment?

Arrive 10-15 minutes before your scheduled time. This gives you time to locate the entrance, go through security, and check in. Arriving too early (30+ minutes) won't help as you'll need to wait outside until your appointment time. Late arrivals may not be accommodated.

Can I track my visa application online?

Yes, use the VFS Global tracking portal with your reference number and passport number. You'll see status updates like "Application Received," "Under Process at Embassy," "Processed," and "Ready for Collection." However, the embassy doesn't provide detailed reasons for processing delays through VFS.

What if my visa is rejected? Will I get a refund?

No, visa fees are non-refundable regardless of the decision. If rejected, you'll receive your passport with a rejection letter explaining the reasons. You can reapply with improved documentation, but you'll need to pay full fees again for the new application.

Do I need an Irish bank account to apply from Dublin?

No, you don't need an Irish bank account. However, if you're residing in Ireland, you must show proof of legal residence (IRP card). For bank statements, you can submit statements from your home country bank if you're a short-term resident in Ireland.

Can I submit applications for multiple Schengen countries at once?

No, each Schengen visa application must be submitted separately to the respective country. If you're visiting multiple countries, apply to the country where you'll spend the most nights. If equal nights, apply to your first point of entry. You cannot hold multiple Schengen visa applications simultaneously.

Is parking available at VFS Dublin?

VFS Global Dublin doesn't have dedicated parking. Street parking on Harcourt Road is limited and metered. The closest car park is Q-Park Harcourt Square (5-minute walk). We strongly recommend using public transport - the Luas Green Line stops right beside the building at Harcourt Street station.

What languages are spoken at VFS Dublin?

All VFS staff speak English. The application process is conducted in English. If you need assistance in another language, you should bring someone who can translate, though VFS may not allow them into the biometrics area. All forms and documents must be in English or officially translated to English.

Can I expedite my visa application at VFS?

VFS offers priority or express services for some countries, but this depends on the embassy's policy and availability. Not all embassies offer expedited processing. If available, you'll see this option when booking your appointment online. Express service typically costs €30-€50 extra and can reduce processing time by 3-5 days.

What happens if I provided incorrect information in my application?

If you notice an error before submission, inform VFS staff immediately during your appointment. They may allow you to make corrections. If the error is discovered after submission, you cannot make changes. Minor typos may be overlooked by the embassy, but significant errors could lead to rejection. Always double-check your form before your appointment.

Accessibility & Special Requirements

VFS Global Dublin is committed to accessibility:

  • Wheelchair Access: Building has elevator access to 1st floor
  • Disabled Parking: Available on Harcourt Road (limited)
  • Assistance: Inform VFS staff if you need additional help during your visit
  • Medical Exemptions: If you cannot provide fingerprints due to medical reasons, bring a medical certificate
  • Sign Language: Not routinely available - bring an interpreter if needed

If you have special requirements, contact VFS at least 48 hours before your appointment to arrange assistance.
