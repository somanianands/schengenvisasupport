France Schengen Visa Guide 2026 — How to Apply via TLScontact, Documents & Tips

📅 Updated: February 2026⏱️ 9 min read

France is the world's most visited country and receives more Schengen visa applications than any other nation. Paris, the French Riviera, the Loire Valley, and the Alps attract millions of visitors annually. Whether you're planning a Paris city break or a road trip through Provence, this guide covers everything you need to successfully apply for a France Schengen visa in 2026.

How to Apply — Step by Step

  1. Determine your VFS/TLScontact centre — France uses TLScontact in most countries. Visit france-visas.gouv.fr to check your country's process.
  2. Book appointment online — TLScontact: tlscontact.com. Allow 4–8 weeks lead time for summer travel.
  3. Complete the application form — Use the France-Visas online portal or the VFS/TLS form. Fill in French or English.
  4. Compile supporting documents (see list below).
  5. Attend appointment — submit documents, provide biometrics, pay fee.
  6. Track application via the France-Visas tracking portal.
  7. Collect passport at the centre or by courier.

Financial Requirements

France recommends €65 per day of stay. For a typical 10-day Paris trip, show at least €650–€800 in your account. Key expectations:

  • Consistently maintained balance across 3 months — not a last-minute deposit
  • Regular salary credits clearly visible
  • Avoid multiple large unexplained transfers just before applying
  • If sponsored, sponsor provides a formal letter, their bank statements, and proof of relationship

France-Specific Tips

  • Apply early for Paris summer trips (June–August). This is peak season globally. TLScontact centres can be heavily booked. Apply 2–3 months ahead if possible.
  • The france-visas.gouv.fr portal is the official application system — always use this for your form, even if submitting via TLScontact.
  • Hotel bookings showing Paris arrondissement addresses strengthen your application. Booking.com or similar is acceptable — full payment not required.
  • Cover letter is strongly recommended. A detailed day-by-day itinerary reflecting Paris landmarks shows genuine travel planning.
  • French consulates are strict on genuineness — if your declared purpose is tourism, your documents should consistently support tourism (not just the visa fee minimum).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is France hard to get a Schengen visa from?

It's moderate — France has an 85–88% approval rate. With complete, consistent documents and genuine travel plans, approval is very achievable. France does receive more applications than any other Schengen country and scrutinises documentation carefully.

How much bank balance do I need?

Approximately €65 per day. For a 10-day trip, show at least €650–€800 across 3 months of consistent statements.

Where do I apply?

Through TLScontact visa centres in most countries. Visit tlscontact.com and select France as your destination country. In some countries you apply at the French consulate directly.

How long does processing take?

15 calendar days standard. June–September can extend to 30–45 days. Apply at least 6–8 weeks before travel for summer trips.

Can I visit other Schengen countries on a France visa?

Yes. A France-issued Schengen visa is valid for all 29 Schengen member states within the 90/180 day limit.
