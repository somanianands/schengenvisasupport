Germany vs Netherlands Schengen Visa — Which Is Easier? (2026)

📅 Updated: February 2026 ⏱️ 7 min read

Germany and the Netherlands are two of Europe's most visited destinations — and two of the most compared consulates by visa applicants planning northern European trips. Here's an honest side-by-side comparison covering approval rates, documentation approach, and processing times.

Head-to-Head Comparison

🇩🇪 Germany

  • Approval rate: ~88–92%
  • Processing: 15–30 days
  • Very thorough documentation review
  • Largest global consular network
  • Strong employment + income scrutiny

🇳🇱 Netherlands

  • Approval rate: ~92–95%
  • Processing: 15 days (consistent)
  • Efficient, clear process
  • Strong VFS infrastructure
  • Business and tourism friendly

Documentation Differences

Germany

German consulates are known for detailed, thorough review. They particularly focus on:

  • Consistency of all documents — any discrepancy triggers further scrutiny
  • Financial document history, not just current balance
  • Purpose of visit must be clearly articulated in the cover letter
  • Employment proof and tax records are taken very seriously

Netherlands

Dutch consulates are efficient and process-focused. They look for:

  • Complete, well-organised application package
  • Clear documentation of financial means
  • Confirmed accommodation and travel plans
  • Netherlands is a business travel hub — business visit applications are viewed positively

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Germany if:

  • Germany is your primary destination
  • You have very strong financial documentation
  • You need the broadest appointment availability globally

Choose Netherlands if:

  • Netherlands / Amsterdam is your primary destination
  • First-time Schengen applicant with good docs
  • You want faster, more consistent processing

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Germany or Netherlands easier for a Schengen visa?

Netherlands has slightly higher approval rates and more consistent processing times. Germany's approval is strong for well-documented applications but involves more rigorous scrutiny.

How long does processing take?

Both aim for 15 calendar days. Germany can stretch to 30 during peak summer. Netherlands is generally faster.

Do they need different documents?

Both use standard Schengen documents. Germany scrutinises financial history more closely. Netherlands focuses on completeness and consistency.

Can I visit both on one Schengen visa?

Yes. Any Schengen visa covers all 27 countries. Apply to whichever you spend the most nights in.

What are the financial requirements?

Both follow the Schengen standard of approximately €50–100 per day. Germany checks the history of your balance more than just the current figure.
