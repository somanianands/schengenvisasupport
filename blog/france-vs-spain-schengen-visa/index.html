France vs Spain Schengen Visa 2026 — Approval Rates, Processing Times & Which Is Easier

📅 Updated: February 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read

If you're going to France — apply to France. If you're going to Spain — apply to Spain. That's the rule, and it's non-negotiable. But if your trip covers both, or you have some flexibility in your itinerary, understanding the differences really does matter. France's refusal rate for Indians is roughly double Spain's. France takes weeks longer to process. The document scrutiny is on a different level. Knowing this before you apply could save you a rejection.

Important: Apply to where you're actually going

You must apply through the embassy of your primary destination — the Schengen country where you'll spend the most nights. If you're splitting time equally, apply through the first country of entry. You cannot choose France or Spain purely for strategic reasons if it's not your primary destination.

Approval Rates: France vs Spain

The EU Commission publishes Schengen visa statistics every year — and the gap between France and Spain is stark. France processes more visa applications than any other Schengen country (~3.5 million a year), and refuses a much higher share of them. Spain is quieter, faster, and more likely to say yes:

What It's Actually Like Applying to Each

🇫🇷 France — Higher Scrutiny

  • ITR / Form 16 routinely requested
  • Bank statements must be original / bank-stamped
  • Cover letter assessed closely
  • Ties to home country assessed rigorously
  • Interview sometimes required for first-timers
  • Appointment slots sell out months ahead

🇪🇸 Spain — More Approachable

  • Standard 3-month bank statements accepted
  • ITR helpful but less critical than for France
  • BLS International process is straightforward
  • Lower refusal rate for first-time applicants
  • No interview typically required
  • Appointment availability relatively better

Frequently Asked Questions

Which has a higher Schengen visa approval rate — France or Spain?

Spain's global approval rate (~92–95%) is significantly higher than France's (~79–83%). Spain's refusal rate for Indian applicants (~8–12%) is roughly half that of France (~21–26%). Approval is not guaranteed through either — your individual circumstances are the primary driver.

Is France or Spain easier for a first-time Schengen visa applicant?

Spain is generally more approachable for first-time applicants. The BLS International process is straightforward, financial requirements are more moderate, and the refusal rate is lower. France has higher document expectations and a history of stricter assessment for first-timers from South Asia.

How long does France vs Spain Schengen processing take?

Spain: typically 2–3 weeks from India, faster in off-peak. France: 3–6 weeks from India year-round, up to 8 weeks in peak summer. Both must legally process in 15 working days, but neither reliably meets this in high-volume periods.

Does France give multiple-entry visas more often than Spain?

France has a higher proportion of multiple-entry visas in its issue statistics, particularly for repeat applicants. Both countries follow the same EU guidelines, so your travel history is the primary determining factor regardless of which you apply through.

Can I apply for both France and Spain on the same trip?

One visa covers the entire Schengen Area. Apply through your primary destination (most nights spent). If equal, apply through first port of entry. One application; one visa; one country responsible.
