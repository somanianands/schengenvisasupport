Schengen Visa Sponsorship Letter Templates 2026 — Copy-Paste Ready

📅 Updated: February 24, 2026 ⏱️ 11 min read

A sponsorship letter (also called an affidavit of support or financial guarantee letter) is required when someone else is covering your travel costs — or when you're visiting a host who is inviting you. Done correctly, a strong sponsorship letter can significantly strengthen an application where the applicant's own finances are limited.

Below are three complete, copy-paste templates covering the most common scenarios, followed by a checklist of what must accompany each one.

What Is a Schengen Visa Sponsorship Letter?

A sponsorship letter is a written declaration from a sponsor — a person who will cover all or part of the applicant's travel, accommodation, and daily expenses during their Schengen visit. It establishes:

  • Who the sponsor is and their relationship to the applicant
  • What expenses the sponsor will cover
  • That the sponsor has the financial capacity to do so
  • That the applicant will return to their home country after the visit

What Must Accompany the Letter

The letter alone is never enough. It must be submitted with:

  • Copy of sponsor's passport or residence permit
  • Sponsor's last 3 months of bank statements
  • Proof of relationship (marriage certificate, birth certificate, friendship photos/correspondence)
  • Proof of accommodation if hosting: property deed + rental agreement, or ownership documents
  • If sponsor is employed: their employment letter or payslips

📄 Template 1: Family Sponsorship Letter

Use when: a parent, sibling, or other family member living abroad is sponsoring your visit or you are visiting them.

[Sponsor's Full Name]
[Sponsor's Full Address]
[City, Country, Postcode]
[Date]

To: The Visa Officer
[Embassy/Consulate Name] Schengen Visa Section

Re: Financial Sponsorship for Schengen Visa Application — [Applicant's Full Name], Passport No. [XXXXXXXX]

I, [Sponsor's Full Name], holder of [Sponsor's Passport/Residence Permit Type and Number], residing at [Sponsor's Full Address], [City, Country], write to confirm that I will financially sponsor the visit of my [relationship — e.g., daughter / son / sibling / parent], [Applicant's Full Name], to [Destination Country] from [Arrival Date] to [Departure Date].

[Applicant's Full Name] currently resides at [Applicant's Home Address], [Country]. S/he holds [Passport Nationality] nationality and is employed as [Applicant's Occupation / Student at X University].

During [his/her] visit, [Applicant's Name] will be hosted at my residence at the above address. I confirm that I will cover all costs related to [his/her] stay, including accommodation, daily living expenses, and any other incidental costs. The total estimated cost of the visit is approximately [€X,XXX].

I am financially capable of covering these expenses, as evidenced by the enclosed bank statements and [employment letter / payslips / business registration]. My current monthly income is approximately [€X,XXX / £X,XXX].

I confirm that [Applicant's Name] will return to [his/her] home country upon the completion of [his/her] authorised stay, and will fully comply with all conditions of the Schengen visa.

Please find attached in support of this letter:

  • Copy of my [passport / residence permit]
  • Last 3 months of bank statements
  • Employment letter / payslips
  • Proof of accommodation (property deed / tenancy agreement)
  • [Relationship proof — e.g., birth certificate, marriage certificate]

Should you require any additional information, please do not hesitate to contact me at [Sponsor's Email] or [Sponsor's Phone Number].

Yours sincerely,

[Sponsor's Signature]
[Sponsor's Full Name]
[Date]

📄 Template 2: Friend / Host Invitation Letter

Use when: a friend living in a Schengen country is hosting you and/or covering costs.

[Host's Full Name]
[Host's Full Address]
[City, Country, Postcode]
[Date]

To: The Visa Officer
[Embassy/Consulate Name] Schengen Visa Section

Re: Invitation and Financial Support for — [Applicant's Full Name], Passport No. [XXXXXXXX]

I, [Host's Full Name], a [nationality] national residing in [City, Country] on [residence permit type / citizen status], wish to formally invite my friend, [Applicant's Full Name], to visit me in [City, Country] from [Arrival Date] to [Departure Date].

[Applicant's Name] and I have known each other for [X years], having met [brief context — e.g., while studying at X University / through mutual colleagues]. We have maintained regular contact and this invitation is for a personal visit.

During [his/her] visit, [Applicant's Name] will be accommodated at my residence: [Host's Full Address]. I confirm that I will cover the cost of [his/her] accommodation during this period. [If covering all costs: "I will also cover all daily living expenses during the visit."/ If not: "The applicant will be responsible for covering [his/her] personal spending and travel costs, with accommodation provided by myself free of charge."]

I confirm that [Applicant's Name] will depart from the Schengen Area before the expiry of the authorised stay and will comply fully with all visa conditions.

Enclosed with this letter are:

  • Copy of my [passport / residence permit]
  • Last 3 months of bank statements
  • Proof of my residence / accommodation address
  • Evidence of our friendship (photos, WhatsApp/email correspondence)

I am happy to provide any further information required.

Yours faithfully,

[Host's Signature]
[Host's Full Name]
[Date]

📄 Template 3: Business / Corporate Sponsorship Letter

Use when: a company (employer or host company) is sponsoring a business trip and covering costs on company letterhead.

[COMPANY LETTERHEAD]

[Date]

To: The Visa Officer
[Embassy/Consulate Name] Schengen Visa Section

Re: Corporate Sponsorship — Business Visit Visa — [Applicant's Full Name]

We, [Company Name], registered at [Company's Full Address], [City, Country] (Company Registration No. [XXXXXXXX]), write to confirm our sponsorship of [Mr./Ms. Applicant's Full Name] for [his/her] business visit to [Destination Country] from [Arrival Date] to [Departure Date].

[Applicant's Name] is [his/her relationship to the company — e.g., an employee of our partner company / a client / a vendor] whom we have invited to attend [meetings / conference / training / negotiations] at our offices.

We confirm that our company will be responsible for all costs associated with [his/her] visit, including:

  • International and local travel expenses
  • Accommodation: [Hotel Name and Address / Our company guest facilities]
  • Meals and daily allowances
  • Any other incidental business expenses

[Applicant's Name] will return to [his/her home country] upon completion of the business engagement. [His/Her] participation is strictly for professional purposes.

We enclose the following documents in support:

  • Company registration certificate
  • Company bank statements or letter of financial standing
  • Meeting invitation / agenda / conference registration
  • Signatory's passport/ID copy

For any further information, please contact us at [Company Email] or [Phone Number].

Yours faithfully,

[Authorised Signatory's Signature]
[Full Name]
[Job Title]
[Company Name]
[Date]

Does a Sponsorship Letter Need to Be Notarised?

It depends on the destination country and your nationality. As a rough guide:

CountryNotarisation requirement
FrancePreferred for family visits; required in some cases — check VFS France requirements
SpainOften requires formal "certificado de empadronamiento" from Spanish host + means declaration
ItalySome consulates request sworn invitation letter for family visits
GermanyNot typically required; strong supporting documents suffice
NetherlandsNot typically required

When in doubt: get it notarised. The cost is small; the peace of mind is significant.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Schengen visa sponsorship letter?

A written declaration from a sponsor confirming they will cover the applicant's travel and accommodation costs. It establishes the relationship between sponsor and applicant, what is being covered, and the sponsor's financial capacity.

Does a sponsor need to be in the Schengen country?

No. A sponsor can be anywhere — family abroad, a friend in the destination country, or an employer. What matters is credibility: proof of relationship, proof of financial capacity, and – if in a Schengen country – proof of their legal status there.

What documents must accompany a sponsorship letter?

Sponsor's passport/residence permit copy, 3 months of the sponsor's bank statements, proof of relationship (marriage/birth certificate or correspondence), and proof of accommodation if the sponsor is hosting you.

Can a sponsorship letter replace my own bank statements?

A strong sponsorship package can reduce the weight placed on your own finances. However, most consulates still prefer to see some personal funds. Never submit a sponsorship letter without the sponsor's bank statements proving their financial capacity.

Does a sponsorship letter need to be notarised?

Depends on the country and nationality. France, Spain and Italy may require it in some cases. Germany and Netherlands generally don't require it. Check the specific embassy requirements, and when in doubt, get it notarised.
