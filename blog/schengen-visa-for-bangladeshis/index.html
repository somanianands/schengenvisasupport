Schengen Visa for Bangladeshis 2026 — Best Countries, Documents & Honest Tips

📅 Updated: February 2026⏱️ 10 min read

Getting a Schengen visa as a Bangladeshi national requires careful preparation. While it's absolutely achievable — thousands of Bangladeshi professionals and tourists visit Europe every year — it requires strong documentation, the right country choice, and an honest, well-organised application. This guide gives you the complete, realistic picture.

Approval Rate Reality for Bangladeshi Applicants

Schengen consulates handle Bangladeshi applications with higher scrutiny than some nationalities due to historical overstay and immigration concern patterns. Rejection rates vary by country but can be 35–55% in some consulates. This does not mean your application will be rejected — it means you need to be more thorough than average.

Best Countries for First-Time Bangladeshi Applicants

Greece and Portugal are the recommended starting points for Bangladeshi nationals applying for their first Schengen visa. Both countries:

  • Have higher overall approval rates
  • Are genuinely attractive tourist destinations for first-timers
  • Require lower daily financial proof (~€50–€65/day)
  • Process applications efficiently

Once you have one Schengen stamp in your passport showing you visited and returned, future applications to any Schengen country — including France and Germany — become significantly easier.

The Transit Visa Requirement for Bangladeshi Nationals

Bangladeshi passport holders require a Schengen Airport Transit Visa (Type A) when connecting through most Schengen airports — even without leaving the terminal. This applies when transiting Frankfurt, Amsterdam, Paris CDG, and others. You are exempt if you hold a valid Schengen visa, a valid US/UK visa, or certain other qualifying permits. Always check the specific requirement for your layover airport and country before booking.

Key Tips for Bangladeshi Applicants

  • Start with Greece or Portugal as your first Schengen country. A successful first Schengen visit stamp opens doors to all future applications.
  • Provide 6 months of bank statements — not just the minimum 3. Consistency over 6 months is far more persuasive.
  • Your TIN certificate is weight-adding proof of legitimate formal income. Always include it.
  • Property ownership in Bangladesh is your strongest return tie — include registered property documents if you own land or a home.
  • Write a strong cover letter explaining your job, your reasons for the trip, your detailed itinerary, and why you will return (family, job, property).
  • Don't apply for too long a stay on a first application. 7–10 days is more credible than 30 days for a first Schengen trip.
  • Previous rejections must be declared. If you've been rejected before, don't hide it — explain in your cover letter what was lacking and how you've addressed it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which Schengen country is best for Bangladeshi applicants?

Greece and Portugal are the most accessible for first-time Bangladeshi applicants. Both have high overall approval rates (91–95%) and are genuine tourist destinations. Netherlands is good for business purposes.

What financial documents do I need?

6 months of bank statements (stamped), TIN certificate, salary certificate, employment letter, payslips, and property/fixed deposit documents as supplementary financial strength. Show approximately €50–75 per day of stay.

Do Bangladeshis need a transit visa for Schengen?

Yes. Bangladeshi nationals require a Type A airport transit visa for most Schengen airports. This is waived with a valid Schengen, UK, or US visa/permit.

How hard is it for Bangladeshis to get a Schengen visa?

It's achievable but requires thorough preparation. Choose Greece or Portugal, provide 6 months of statements, include TIN and employment docs, and write a strong cover letter. A well-prepared application has a good chance of approval.

What is the NID requirement?

Your National Identity Card isn't on the official checklist but is strongly recommended as it confirms your identity and Bangladesh address. The TIN certificate is even more useful for demonstrating formal income and tax registration.
