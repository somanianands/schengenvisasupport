Spain Schengen Visa Guide 2026 — How to Apply, Documents & Tips

📅 Updated: February 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read

Spain is one of the most visited countries in Europe and one of the most applied-to Schengen visa destinations. From Barcelona to Madrid, the Costa del Sol to the Balearic Islands — millions of visitors apply for a Spain Schengen visa each year. This complete guide covers the full application process, all required documents, how long it takes, and what makes Spanish consulates approve applications.

Who Needs a Spain Schengen Visa?

Citizens of non-EU countries that don't have visa-free access to the Schengen Area need a Spain Schengen visa. This includes nationals of India, Pakistan, Philippines, Nigeria, Bangladesh, China, and most African, South Asian, and Southeast Asian countries.

Citizens of the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and most Latin American countries can visit Spain visa-free for up to 90 days (subject to the 90/180 day rule) — though from 2025 they will need ETIAS pre-travel authorisation.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Determine your consulate. Apply at the Spanish consulate or embassy serving your area, or through VFS Global where available.
  2. Book your appointment. Appointments should be booked 4–6 weeks before you need to submit (or 6–8 weeks during summer).
  3. Gather all documents (detailed list below).
  4. Attend your biometric appointment. Bring all originals and copies. Fingerprints and photo are taken.
  5. Pay the fee. €90 (plus VFS service fee if applicable).
  6. Await decision. Standard 15 calendar days.
  7. Collect your passport. Via VFS or consulate.

Financial Requirements for Spain

Spain's consulate guidelines specify approximately:

  • €100 per day of your visit (if you have pre-paid accommodation)
  • €125 per day if accommodation is not pre-paid
  • Minimum €900 for any visit regardless of duration

These are guidelines, not rigid cut-offs. Bank statements showing a consistent, healthy balance over 3 months are more convincing than one large recent deposit.

Spain-Specific Tips

  • Spanish consulates respond well to tourism-focused applications. An itinerary visiting popular regions (Barcelona, Madrid, Seville, Costa del Sol) is easy to document.
  • Apply to Spain directly, not to a "nearby" Schengen country. Spanish consular officers can tell when the destination doesn't match the consulate.
  • Book accommodation covering all nights — unfilled nights in your itinerary raise questions.
  • Summer applications (June–August) should be submitted at least 6–8 weeks in advance due to the high volume.
  • Spain is one of the more multiple-entry-friendly Schengen countries for repeating visitors with clean travel history.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I apply for a Spain Schengen visa?

Book an appointment at the Spanish consulate or VFS Global centre, gather all documents, attend your biometric appointment, pay the €90 fee, and wait 15 calendar days for a decision.

What documents are required?

Application form, valid passport, 2 photos, travel insurance (€30,000+), flight reservation, hotel booking, bank statements, employment letter, payslips, and cover letter.

What is Spain's approval rate?

~89–93% overall. Spain is one of the more accessible Schengen destinations for tourism visas with clear, consistent requirements.

How long does Spain visa processing take?

15 calendar days standard. Up to 30 days during June–August peak season. Apply at least 6 weeks before your travel date.

What is the Spain Schengen visa fee?

€90 for adults. €45 for children 6–12. Free under 6. VFS service fee additional. Non-refundable.
