Austria Schengen Visa Guide 2026 — Apply, Fees, Documents & Tips

📅 Updated: February 2026⏱️ 8 min read

Austria is one of Europe's most beautiful and culturally rich destinations — home to Vienna's imperial palaces, Salzburg's Mozart heritage, the Alps, and the fairytale village of Hallstatt. Applying for an Austrian Schengen visa is straightforward if you know the process. This guide covers everything from document requirements to insider tips for a successful application in 2026.

Austria for Business Travellers

Vienna is one of Europe's major business hubs — hosting UN offices, EU institutions, and major international conferences. Business visa applicants need:

  • An official invitation letter from the Austrian company or organisation
  • Letter from your own employer confirming the business purpose and sponsorship (if applicable)
  • Conference or event registration (if attending an event)
  • Business bank statement or company financials for self-employed

Financial Proof — What Austria Expects

Austria requires you to demonstrate a minimum of approximately €70 per day for your stay. For a typical 10-day trip, aim to show at least €1,500–€2,000 in your bank account, maintained over 3 months.

⚠️ Tip: Include all sources of funds — salary, savings, rental income, investments. Austrian consulates are thorough reviewers; a clear, well-documented financial picture strengthens your case significantly.

Tips for a Successful Austria Visa Application

  • Write a detailed cover letter. Austrian consulates appreciate thorough documentation. A clear cover letter with day-by-day itinerary, trip purpose, and financial statement summary adds significant credibility.
  • Book refundable accommodation/flights. You only need reservations — not final paid bookings — at the time of application.
  • Provide at least 3 months of bank statements — bank-stamped (not internet printouts).
  • Combine Austria with other Schengen countries if applicable — one visa covers the whole Schengen area.
  • Apply early for Vienna or Salzburg in December — Christmas markets are extremely popular and VFS gets very busy.
🛑 Common Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Submitting bank statements without a bank stamp — digital printouts alone are usually rejected
  • Not including return flight reservations alongside outbound flights
  • Incomplete application forms — every field must be filled
  • Booking non-refundable full-payment flights/hotels before the visa is granted

Frequently Asked Questions

Who handles Austria visa applications?

VFS Global handles Austria visa applications in most countries. In some countries, OeSD or the Austrian embassy/consulate directly handles applications. Check the Austrian MFA website or VFS Global for your specific country.

What is the Austria Schengen visa fee?

€90 for adults (children 6–12: €45; under 6: free) as per EU Schengen rules. Plus VFS service fee of ~€30–45. There is no visa fee refund if the application is refused.

How long does Austria visa processing take?

Standard processing is 15 calendar days. Allow up to 45 days during peak seasons. Apply at least 6 weeks before travel — 8–10 weeks in summer or at Christmas.

How much money do I need for Austria?

Approximately €70 per day. For a 10-day trip, show €1,500–€2,000 in a consistently maintained bank account over 3 months.

Can I travel to other Schengen countries with an Austria Schengen visa?

Yes. An Austrian Schengen visa is valid for all 29 Schengen member states. The 90/180 day rule applies across all Schengen countries combined.
