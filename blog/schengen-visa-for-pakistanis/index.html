Schengen Visa for Pakistani Passport Holders 2026 — Complete Guide

📅 Updated: February 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read

Pakistani passport holders face one of the higher Schengen refusal rates globally — but that doesn't mean approval is impossible. Applicants who invest time in building a complete, consistent, well-documented application get approved every day. This guide explains exactly what Pakistani applicants need, where to apply, and what the consulate is really looking for.

Why Pakistani Applications Are Scrutinised More

Pakistan has a historically high rate of Schengen visa refusals because consulates assess irregular migration risk carefully. The central questions an officer asks for any Pakistani application: Does this person have strong financial ties to Pakistan? Is there a clear reason to return? Is the financial documentation consistent and credible?

Addressing each of these directly — with evidence — dramatically improves approval chances.

Documents Pakistani Applicants Must Prioritise

Financial Documents

  • Bank statements: Personal account, last 3–6 months, bank-stamped. Aim for a consistent healthy balance throughout — large deposits immediately before applying look suspicious.
  • NTN Certificate / FBR Tax Returns: Proof of declared income in Pakistan. This is the equivalent of India's ITR and is taken seriously by European consulates.
  • Salary certificate: From employer confirming monthly salary.
  • Business bank statements: If self-employed, 6 months of business account transactions.

Employment Documents

  • Detailed employment letter: organisation, post, salary, leave approval, confirmation of return
  • Last 3–6 months payslips
  • If self-employed: business registration certificate, NTN, business bank statements

Home Country Ties

  • Property ownership documents (especially land or real estate)
  • Family ties remaining in Pakistan
  • Previous approved international visas (UK, USA, GCC, Australia)
  • Business or directorship evidence

Applying from the UAE, Saudi Arabia, or Qatar

Large numbers of Pakistani nationals reside in GCC countries. If you legally reside in the UAE, KSA, or Qatar, you apply for the Schengen visa from your country of residence. You'll need:

  • Valid GCC residency visa / Iqama
  • Employment contract and payslips from your GCC employer
  • NOC from GCC employer
  • GCC bank statements (3–6 months)
  • Pakistan home account statements (strength document)

See our GCC Residents Schengen Visa Guide for full details.

Improving Your Chances After a Rejection

If you've been rejected before, read our guide on how previous rejections affect new applications. In brief:

  • Always declare previous refusals — lying will result in permanent bans
  • Address the stated reason for refusal explicitly in your cover letter
  • Wait at least 3 months before reapplying unless circumstances have genuinely changed
  • Strengthen the specific weak area — usually financial proof

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Pakistani citizens get a Schengen visa?

Yes. Pakistani passport holders can and do receive Schengen visas. The process requires careful documentation showing financial stability and strong home-country ties.

What is the Schengen visa rejection rate for Pakistanis?

Historically 30–40%, well above the global average (~15%). The primary reasons are weak financial proof, insufficient home-country ties, and incomplete applications. Strong documentation dramatically narrows this gap.

Which Schengen country is easiest for Pakistanis?

Sweden, Portugal, and Finland have shown better approval rates. But always apply to the country you're genuinely visiting most.

Can Pakistanis in the UAE apply for Schengen?

Yes. Apply from the UAE using your UAE residency documents, employer NOC, UAE bank statements, and supporting Pakistan income documentation.

What documents are most important for Pakistani applicants?

NTN/FBR tax returns, bank-stamped personal bank statements (showing healthy consistent balance), detailed employment letter, payslips, and property/asset documents showing Pakistan ties.
