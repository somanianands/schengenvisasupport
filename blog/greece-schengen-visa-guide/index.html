Greece Schengen Visa Guide 2026 — How to Apply, Documents & Expert Tips

📅 Updated: February 2026⏱️ 9 min read

Greece is one of the most popular summer destinations in the world — and one of the best Schengen visa choices for applicants seeking high approval odds. Santorini's sunsets, the Acropolis in Athens, Mykonos's beaches, and the history of Crete draw millions of visitors annually. Greece's visa approval rate of approximately 91–95% makes it especially attractive for Indian, Filipino, and Asian applicants. Here's your complete 2026 guide.

Why Greece Is an Excellent Schengen Choice

  • Highest approval rate among major tourist destinations — 91–95% overall; even higher for well-documented applications
  • Tourism-friendly consulates — Greece actively encourages tourism-based applications; consulate officers understand the genuine tourism market
  • Lower financial threshold — approx. €50–€65/day compared to €75–€100/day in some northern European countries
  • Multiple entry visas often granted — applicants with clean travel history frequently receive multiple-entry visas valid for 1–5 years
  • Visa valid across all Schengen — use it to explore 28 other Schengen countries beyond Greece

Step-by-Step Application Guide

  1. Find your VFS centre for Greece — VFS Global handles most Greece applications worldwide. Visit vfsglobal.com → select Greece.
  2. Book appointment early — Greek summer (June–August) is extremely popular. Book VFS appointments 8–10 weeks before travel for summer; 4–6 weeks for other seasons.
  3. Complete the Schengen visa application form — Download the official form from the Greek consulate or VFS. Fill completely, sign every page, and date it.
  4. Prepare all documents — Follow the checklist below. Organise neatly with a covering summary sheet.
  5. Attend VFS appointment — Arrive 10 minutes early. Submit documents, give biometrics (first-time or every 59 months), pay the visa fee.
  6. Track your application — VFS provides a SMS/email tracking reference. Decisions usually come within 15 days.
  7. Collect visa — In person or via VFS courier service.

Financial Requirements

Greece requires approximately €50–€65 per day of your stay — lower than Germany, France, or Switzerland. For a 7-night trip to the Greek islands, show around €500–€600 minimum, preferably more. Key guidance:

  • 3 months of consistent bank statements — Greek consulates look for regular salary deposits and a stable maintained balance
  • Savings accounts are fine — a consistent savings balance is as good as a current account with salary
  • Large unexplained deposits are red flags — if a relative transferred money just before you applied, this will be questioned
  • Sponsored trips: sponsor must provide an invitation letter, their own 3 months of bank statements, their address proof, and proof of your relationship
  • Self-employed and freelancers: provide 6 months of bank statements, tax returns, and proof of business registration (see our self-employed guide)

Sample Greece Itinerary (Helps Your Application)

A specific, credible itinerary in your cover letter significantly strengthens a Greece application. Here's an example structure for a 10-day trip:

Day 1: Arrive Athens. Check in [Hotel name, address]. Explore Plaka neighbourhood.

Day 2: Acropolis, Parthenon, Acropolis Museum. Dinner in Monastiraki.

Day 3: Athens sightseeing — Ancient Agora, Temple of Hephaestus. Evening ferry to Santorini.

Days 4–6: Santorini. [Hotel name, Oia/Fira]. Caldera views, Akrotiri ruins, black sand beaches.

Days 7–9: Mykonos. [Hotel name]. Windmills, Little Venice, beaches.

Day 10: Return flight from Athens.

This level of specificity — with hotel names and addresses already researched — is far stronger than "I plan to visit Athens and some islands."

Greece Island vs Mainland — Which to Apply For?

If you're visiting both Athens and the islands, your application is to Greece regardless. All of Greece — mainland, Santorini, Mykonos, Crete, Rhodes — is Schengen territory and uses the same Greek consulate process. You don't need separate applications for different Greek islands.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Greece easy to get a Schengen visa from?

Yes — Greece has a 91–95% approval rate, one of the highest in Schengen. Particularly strong record for Indian, Filipino, and Asian applicants. Well-documented tourism applications are routinely approved.

How much bank balance do I need?

Approximately €50–€65 per day of stay. For a week in Greece, show €500–€600+ in consistent bank statements over 3 months.

When should I apply?

4–6 weeks before travel for non-peak periods. For summer (June–August) Greek island trips, apply 8–10 weeks ahead. VFS slots fill up fast in summer.

Can I visit other Schengen countries on a Greece visa?

Yes. A Greek Schengen visa is valid for all 29 Schengen states within the 90/180 day rule.

What documents do I need?

Application form, valid passport, biometric photos, travel insurance, return flight reservation, hotel bookings, 3-month bank statements, employment letter, payslips, and a cover letter with detailed itinerary.
