🇷🇴 Romania Schengen Visa Guide 2026

Updated: February 1, 2026 · 15 min read

Romania officially joined the Schengen Area on March 31, 2024 (air/sea borders) and January 1, 2025 (land borders). With only €50 per day financial requirement - one of the lowest in Schengen - Romania offers an accessible entry point for your European travel plans.

Romania Visa Requirements (2026)

Financial Requirements

Romania requires proof of €50 per day for your stay. Here's what that means in practice:
5 days €250 €350-400 €325 (save €75!)
7 days €350 €500-550 €455 (save €105!)
10 days €500 €700-800 €650 (save €150!)
15 days €750 €1,000-1,100 €975 (save €225!)
30 days €1,500 €2,000-2,200 €1,950 (save €450!)
💡 Smart Strategy: While Romania only requires €50/day minimum, showing €60-70/day makes your application stronger. It demonstrates financial comfort and planning, not just bare minimum compliance. For a 7-day trip, aim for €500-550 in your account.

Complete Document Checklist

  1. Visa application form
    • Download from Romanian embassy website
    • Complete in English or Romanian
    • Sign and date in black ink
    • Don't leave any fields blank (write "N/A" if not applicable)
  2. Valid passport
    • Valid for at least 3 months after your planned return date
    • Issued within the last 10 years
    • At least 2 blank pages for visa stamps
    • Photocopy of all pages with stamps/visas
  3. Passport-size photos (2 photos, 35x45mm, white background, taken within last 6 months)
  4. Travel health insurance
    • €30,000 minimum coverage
    • Valid for all Schengen countries
    • Covers emergency medical care, hospitalization, repatriation
  5. Flight reservations
    • Round-trip bookings showing entry and exit dates
    • Don't purchase actual tickets until visa approved!
    • Use airline's "hold booking" option or travel agent reservation
  6. Accommodation proof
    • Hotel bookings for entire stay (use free cancellation options)
    • OR invitation letter if staying with friends/family (notarized)
    • Must cover every single night of your trip
  7. Bank statements
    • Last 3-6 months of transactions
    • MUST be stamped and signed by bank
    • Show regular income deposits
    • Sufficient balance for trip duration
  8. Employment documentation
    • Employment letter on company letterhead (stating position, salary, employment duration)
    • Last 3 months of salary slips
    • Leave approval letter from employer
    • If self-employed: business registration, tax returns, bank statements
  9. Cover letter (explaining trip purpose, itinerary, ties to home country)
  10. Detailed travel itinerary (day-by-day plan with places, activities, hotels)

Step-by-Step Application Guide

Step 1: Prepare Documents (2-3 weeks before travel)

  • Visit bank to get stamped statements
  • Request employment letter from HR department
  • Purchase travel insurance online
  • Book hotels with free cancellation policy
  • Get flight reservation (not actual ticket)
  • Write detailed cover letter
  • Create day-by-day itinerary

Step 2: Schedule Appointment (book 2-4 weeks in advance)

  • Visit Romanian embassy/consulate website for your country
  • Book appointment online or by phone
  • Morning appointments often better (visa officers fresher, less rushed)
  • Bring all documents organized in a folder

Step 3: Attend Visa Appointment

  • Arrive 15-20 minutes early
  • Bring original documents + photocopies of everything
  • Pay visa fee: €90 for adults, €45 for children 6-12 years
  • Submit biometric data (fingerprints, photo)
  • Answer interview questions clearly and honestly
  • Keep receipt and tracking number

Step 4: Wait for Processing (10-15 days typical)

  • Standard processing: 10-15 working days
  • Can extend to 30-45 days if additional verification needed
  • Track application status online if available
  • Embassy may contact you for additional documents

Step 5: Collect Your Passport

  • Pick up in person or arrange courier delivery (check embassy policy)
  • Verify visa details immediately: dates, spelling of name, validity period
  • If approved, check entry/exit dates match your plans
  • If rejected, read rejection letter carefully for specific reasons

Approval Tips for Romania Visa

Romania-Specific Advantages

🎯 Why Romania Applications Often Succeed:
  • Realistic requirements: €50/day reflects Romania's actual cost of living
  • Tourism-friendly: Romania actively promotes tourism to boost economy
  • Less overwhelmed embassies: Not as backlogged as France/Germany/Spain
  • New member enthusiasm: Proving they can handle Schengen responsibilities efficiently

Strengthen Your Application

  1. Show genuine interest in Romania:
    • Mention specific Romanian attractions: Bran Castle, Peles Castle, Brasov Old Town, Danube Delta
    • Reference Romanian culture, history, or traditions
    • Don't just list Romania as "gateway to Europe" - that raises red flags
    • Research Romanian cities you'll visit and mention local festivals or events
  2. Demonstrate strong home ties:
    • Employment: 2+ years at current job ideal (shows stability)
    • Family: Spouse, children staying home (marriage certificate, birth certificates)
    • Property: House/apartment ownership documents
    • Financial: Regular income, savings, investments
    • Social: Community involvement, memberships, commitments
  3. Financial documentation beyond minimum:
    • Show €60-70/day instead of just €50/day
    • 6 months of bank statements better than 3 months
    • Regular salary deposits more important than high balance
    • Avoid sudden large deposits week before application (suspicious)
  4. Previous travel history helps:
    • Copies of old visas (USA, UK, Canada, Australia especially valuable)
    • Passport stamps showing you returned from previous trips
    • Demonstrates you're a responsible traveler

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Generic cover letter ("I want to visit Europe") - be specific about Romania
  • ❌ Fake hotel bookings - use real bookings with free cancellation
  • ❌ Bank statements without bank stamp - automatic rejection risk
  • ❌ Vague itinerary - "sightseeing" isn't good enough, name actual places
  • ❌ Applying too early (more than 3 months before) or too late (less than 2 weeks)
  • ❌ Inconsistencies between documents (dates don't match, conflicting information)
  • ❌ Poor quality photos - must meet exact specifications

Why Visit Romania?

Romania is one of Europe's most underrated destinations, offering incredible value and authentic experiences:

Top Romanian Destinations

  • 🏰 Transylvania: Bran Castle ("Dracula's Castle"), Brasov medieval town, Sibiu old town, Saxon fortified churches
  • 🏔️ Carpathian Mountains: Hiking, skiing in winter, stunning scenery, traditional mountain villages
  • 🏛️ Bucharest: Palace of Parliament (world's 2nd largest building), Old Town, parks, museums
  • 🌊 Black Sea Coast: Constanta, beach resorts, ancient Roman ruins
  • 🦅 Danube Delta: UNESCO World Heritage Site, birdwatching paradise, boat tours
  • 🎨 Maramures: Traditional wooden churches, rural life, authentic culture

Cost of Travel in Romania

Romania is very affordable, making the €50/day requirement quite comfortable:

  • Budget hotel/hostel: €15-30/night
  • Mid-range hotel: €35-70/night
  • Restaurant meal: €5-12
  • Local transport: €0.50-1.50
  • Museum entry: €3-8
  • Coffee: €1-2.50
  • Beer: €1.50-3

You can genuinely have a great trip on €50/day in Romania - unlike Western Europe where minimum requirements often feel tight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I visit other Schengen countries with a Romania visa?

Absolutely! A Romanian Schengen visa grants you access to all 29 Schengen countries. You can fly into Bucharest, spend a few days in Romania, then travel to Hungary, Austria, Italy - anywhere in the Schengen zone. Just ensure Romania is either your main destination (most nights) or your first entry point into Schengen.

Is Romania fully in Schengen now?

Yes! Romania joined the Schengen Area on March 31, 2024 for air and sea borders, and January 1, 2025 for land borders. This means no passport checks when traveling between Romania and other Schengen countries - you can drive or fly freely once you're in.

Do I need to speak Romanian?

No. Visa applications can be submitted in English. In tourist areas, many people speak English. Your cover letter and documents should be in English (Romanian translations not required unless specifically requested by the embassy).

How long does Romania visa processing take?

Standard processing is 10-15 working days. Can extend to 30-45 days if they need to verify information or request additional documents. Apply 3-4 weeks before your planned trip to have a comfortable buffer.

Should I apply to Romania if I'm mainly visiting other countries?

Only if Romania is either: 1) Your main destination (most nights), OR 2) Your first entry point with roughly equal nights elsewhere. Don't try to "game the system" by applying to Romania just because it's easier - embassies verify itineraries and will reject applications where the main destination is clearly elsewhere.

What if I get rejected?

Don't panic! Most rejections are fixable. Check the rejection code on your letter (Code 1, 2, 3, etc.), understand what went wrong, fix those specific issues, and reapply. See our complete rejection recovery guide for detailed solutions to each rejection reason.
