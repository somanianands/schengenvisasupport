🏨 Hotel Booking for Schengen Visa

You need accommodation proof for your visa, but you shouldn't pay upfront before getting approved. Here's how to show hotel bookings safely.

What Embassies Require

  • Accommodation for entire stay - every night covered
  • Hotel confirmation with booking reference
  • Guest names matching passport
  • Hotel address in Schengen country
  • Check-in/check-out dates

Best Ways to Get Hotel Booking

1. Free Cancellation Bookings (Recommended)

Book hotels with free cancellation policy:

  • Booking.com - many free cancellation options (recommended)
  • Hotels.com - flexible booking policies
  • Expedia - refundable rates available
  • Cancel free if visa rejected (check cancellation deadline)

Book Free Cancellation Hotels →

2. Pay at Property Options

  • Reserve now, pay when you arrive
  • No upfront payment required
  • Still get valid confirmation for visa

3. Staying with Friends/Family

If staying with someone in Europe, you need:

  • Invitation letter from host
  • Host's proof of accommodation (lease/ownership)
  • Host's ID or residence permit copy

What Your Hotel Confirmation Should Show

  • ✅ Hotel name and full address
  • ✅ Booking confirmation number
  • ✅ Guest name(s) - all travelers
  • ✅ Check-in and check-out dates
  • ✅ Room type and number of guests
  • ✅ Hotel contact information

Important Tips

  1. Cover all nights - no gaps in accommodation
  2. Match your itinerary - hotels should align with travel plan
  3. Book in correct countries - show where you'll actually stay
  4. Note cancellation policy - know the deadline
  5. Print confirmation clearly - legible for submission

Airbnb for Schengen Visa?

Yes, Airbnb bookings are generally accepted:

  • Print the booking confirmation email
  • Should show host address clearly
  • Choose "free cancellation" options
  • Some embassies prefer traditional hotels - check requirements

Multiple Cities?

If visiting multiple cities, show accommodation for each:

  • Paris: Hotel A (Day 1-3)
  • Rome: Hotel B (Day 4-6)
  • Barcelona: Hotel C (Day 7-10)

Each booking should be clearly labeled and match your travel itinerary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I book hotels before getting my visa?

Yes, but always use free cancellation or pay-at-property options. Never pay fully non-refundable hotel rates before your visa is approved. Most embassies accept reservation confirmations — they do not require proof of payment.

What if I’m staying with a friend or family?

If staying at a private residence, you need a letter from your host confirming they are hosting you, their address, their contact details, and their relationship to you. This is called an invitation letter or letter of hospitality. Your host may also need to provide their residence permit (if in the EU) or proof of residency.

Do I need bookings for every single night?

Yes. The accommodation must cover all nights of your stated trip duration. A gap of even one night raises questions. If you’re visiting multiple cities, ensure accommodation is booked and listed in your itinerary for each city individually.

Which platforms are accepted for hotel reservations?

Booking.com, Hotels.com, Expedia, Agoda, Airbnb, and direct hotel confirmations are all widely accepted. Airbnb is generally accepted but some older consulates (particularly German) may prefer traditional hotel bookings. When in doubt, use Booking.com with free cancellation.

How far in advance should I book hotels?

Book as close to your appointment as practical so cancellation is easy if rejected. Most visa appointments have a 2–4 week lead time — book hotels a few days before the appointment so they’re fresh and valid on submission day. Booking.com free cancellation bookings remain valid for weeks after booking.
