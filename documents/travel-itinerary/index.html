What is a Travel Itinerary for Schengen Visa?

A travel itinerary is a detailed, day-by-day plan of your trip showing:

  • 📅 Daily schedule - What you'll do each day
  • 📍 Destinations - Cities and places you'll visit
  • 🏨 Accommodations - Where you'll stay each night
  • 🎫 Activities - Attractions, tours, events
  • 🚂 Transportation - How you'll move between cities
⚠️ Important: A travel itinerary is NOT the same as flight tickets. It's your planned activities and destinations during your trip. Flight reservations are a separate document.

What to Include in Your Itinerary

Essential Information
Your Information Full name, passport number at top
Trip Dates Start and end dates (must match visa application)
Main Destination Country where you'll spend most days
Daily Breakdown Day-by-day plan with dates
Accommodations Hotel names and addresses for each night
Activities Specific places you'll visit (museums, landmarks)
Transportation Flights, trains between cities

Level of Detail Required

💡 Perfect Balance:
Be detailed but realistic. You don't need to plan every hour, but show major activities. For example:
  • ✅ Good: "Visit Eiffel Tower and Louvre Museum"
  • ✅ Good: "Morning: Arc de Triomphe; Afternoon: Seine River cruise"
  • ❌ Too vague: "Sightseeing in Paris"
  • ❌ Too detailed: "09:00 - Wake up, 09:30 - Breakfast at Café XYZ, 10:15 - Walk to museum..."

Pro Tips for Creating Your Itinerary

✅ Do's

  • Be realistic - Don't plan to visit 10 cities in 7 days
  • Include specific attractions - "Louvre Museum" not just "sightseeing"
  • Match your cover letter - Ensure dates and destinations align
  • Show main destination clearly - Embassy needs to see you qualify
  • Include hotel addresses - Complete with street address, postal code
  • Add transportation details - Train/flight numbers between cities
  • Be consistent - All documents should show same dates/places

❌ Don'ts

  • Don't be too vague - "Touring Europe" is not enough
  • Don't overplan - No need for hourly schedule
  • Don't contradict yourself - If cover letter says 10 days France, itinerary shouldn't show 5
  • Don't include impossible schedules - Paris to Rome in 3 hours isn't realistic
  • Don't skip days - Account for every single day of your trip

Common Questions While Planning

How detailed should activities be?

Moderate detail is best. Example for one day:

  • ✅ Good: "Morning: Louvre Museum; Afternoon: Eiffel Tower; Evening: Seine cruise"
  • ❌ Too basic: "Paris sightseeing"
  • ❌ Too detailed: "09:00 Wake up, 09:30 Breakfast at X, 10:15 Metro to Y, 10:45 Enter museum..."

Should I book everything before visa approval?

No! Use flexible/refundable reservations:

  • ✅ Flight reservations (not tickets - use reservation services)
  • ✅ Hotel bookings with free cancellation
  • ✅ Pre-book major attractions if they require it (refundable)

Flight Booking vs Travel Itinerary - What's the Difference?
Purpose Shows daily activities & plans Proves entry/exit dates
Format Day-by-day document you create Airline reservation confirmation
Content Hotels, attractions, cities, activities Flight numbers, dates, routes
Required? Strongly recommended (sometimes required) Mandatory
When to get Create before application Reserve (don't buy) before application

Both are separate documents: Your application typically needs BOTH a detailed travel itinerary AND flight reservations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a travel itinerary mandatory for Schengen visa?

It depends on the embassy, but it's highly recommended for all applications. Some embassies (Spain, France) explicitly require it. Even if not mandatory, it significantly strengthens your application by showing you're a prepared, genuine tourist.

Can I change my itinerary after getting the visa?

Yes, your itinerary is a PLAN, not a binding contract. Embassy understands plans can change. However, you should still:

  • Enter/exit on similar dates (±1-2 days okay)
  • Spend most time in the country that issued your visa (main destination rule)
  • Stay within Schengen Area as approved
Major changes (e.g., applying for France but only visiting Greece) can cause issues at border control.

Do I need to book hotels before visa approval?

You need hotel RESERVATIONS, not paid bookings. Use Booking.com or hotels with FREE CANCELLATION. Make reservations, get confirmation emails, and submit those. If visa is rejected, cancel for free. Never pay non-refundable bookings before visa approval.

How do I show "main destination" in multi-country trip?

Count the nights in each country. Example:

  • France: 5 nights ← Main destination (apply to French embassy)
  • Germany: 3 nights
  • Italy: 2 nights
In your itinerary, clearly show France has most days. Apply to French embassy/consulate.

Should I include prices in the itinerary?

Optional but helpful. You can add a summary section with estimated costs:

  • Accommodation: €900
  • Food: €500
  • Transportation: €200
  • Attractions: €150
  • Total: €1,750
This shows you've budgeted realistically and have sufficient funds.
