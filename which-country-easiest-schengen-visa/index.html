Which Country Gives the Easiest Schengen Visa? 🎯

Updated: February 12, 2026 · 18 min read

Short answer: It depends on your profile. But if we're looking at pure statistics, Lithuania, Estonia, and Finland have the highest approval rates (95-97%), while Greece, France, and Iceland offer the best combination of high approval rates and realistic travel plans. This comprehensive guide analyzes approval rates, financial requirements, and processing times to help you choose strategically.

⚠️ Critical Rule:

You must apply to the country that is your main destination (where you'll spend most nights) or first entry point. "Visa shopping" (applying to an easy country when you're actually visiting elsewhere) can lead to rejection and future bans.

How We Compare Countries

When determining which country is "easiest," we evaluate four key factors:

1. Approval Rate

Percentage of visa applications approved. Data from official Schengen statistics 2024-2026.

2. Financial Requirements

Daily minimum funds required. Ranges from €30/day (Estonia) to €120/day (Italy).

3. Processing Time

How quickly decisions are made. Faster processing = less stress.

4. Practical Feasibility

Can you realistically plan a trip there? High approval rate means nothing if you can't justify visiting that country.

💡 Smart Strategy: Don't just pick the "easiest." Pick a country you genuinely want to visit and that matches your travel plans. Authentic trips have higher approval rates than forced itineraries.

Best Country for Your Profile

👨‍💼 Strong Financial Profile (Stable Job, Good Income)

Best choices: France, Greece, Spain, Italy - any country you actually want to visit.

Why: With strong finances and employment, you'll likely get approved anywhere. Focus on where you genuinely want to go.

💰 Limited Budget (Students, Entry-Level Workers)

Best choices: Estonia (€30/day), Lithuania (€40/day), Greece (€50/day)

Why: Lower financial requirements make approval more likely with limited funds.

Example: €600 in savings gets you 20 days in Estonia but only 5 days in Italy.

📄 First-Time Schengen Applicant

Best choices: Greece (88% approval), Lithuania (97% approval)

Why: High approval rates give you better odds. Once you have one successful Schengen visa, future applications become much easier.

Strategy: Get your first visa from an easy country, comply perfectly, then apply to stricter countries with a clean travel history.

🚫 Previous Rejection or Weak Profile

Best choices: Lithuania, Estonia, Finland (highest approval rates)

Why: These countries have more lenient standards and give applications more individual attention.

Important: Fix the issues from your previous rejection first. Don't just "visa shop."

👥 Family/Group Travel

Best choices: Greece, France, Spain (popular family destinations)

Why: Easy to justify, lots of tourist infrastructure, family-friendly reputation.

Avoid: Baltic countries might be harder to justify for family beach vacations.

💼 Business Travelers

Best choices: Germany, France, Netherlands

Why: Major business hubs with smooth processes for business travelers.

Tip: Business visas often have different (sometimes easier) requirements than tourist visas.

🎒 Multi-Country Trip Planning

Best choice: Country where you'll spend most nights or first entry

Strategy:

  • Count nights in each country
  • Apply to the country with most nights
  • If tied, apply to first entry country
  • Document entire itinerary clearly

Example: France (4 nights) → Germany (3 nights) → Italy (4 nights) = Apply to France (tied, first entry)

Strategic Application Tips

1. The "Main Destination" Rule

You MUST apply to:

  • a) The country where you'll spend most nights, OR
  • b) Your first entry point (if nights are equal)
⚠️ Visa Shopping = Rejection:

Applying to Lithuania because it's "easy" but actually planning to spend all your time in France? That's fraud. Consulates share information. You'll get rejected and possibly banned.

2. How to Choose When Planning Multi-Country Trips

Strategy A: Make the easy country your main destination (legitimate)

  • Plan 8 days in Greece, 4 days in Italy, 2 days in France
  • Apply to Greece (main destination)
  • Benefit from Greece's higher approval rate legitimately

Strategy B: Include an easy country legitimately

  • Plan 6 days Paris, 7 days Baltic tour (3 Lithuania + 2 Latvia + 2 Estonia)
  • Apply to Lithuania (most nights in single country)
  • Still visit France, but gained from easier Lithuanian approval

3. Building a Travel History

Long-term strategy:

  1. First visa: Apply to easy country (Greece, Lithuania) for 7-10 day trip
  2. Comply perfectly: Enter/exit as stated, no overstays, use accommodations booked
  3. Future visas: Now you have Schengen history - apply to harder countries with confidence
💡 Pro Tip: A clean Schengen visa history is like credit history. One successful visa from Greece makes your next France or Germany application MUCH easier.

4. When NOT to Choose Based on "Easiest"

  • ❌ If you have to fabricate interest in that country
  • ❌ If you can't afford to actually go there (rejection for false intent)
  • ❌ If your cover letter sounds forced ("I suddenly discovered my passion for Lithuanian history")
  • ❌ If you're changing plans drastically just to game the system

Common Myths Debunked

Myth 1: "I can apply to any Schengen country, they're all the same"

Reality: NO. You must apply to your main destination. Border officers can deny entry if your actual plans don't match the visa you obtained.

Myth 2: "Smaller countries are always easier"

Reality: Partially true. Baltic countries (Lithuania, Estonia, Latvia) have high approval rates, but countries like Monaco or Liechtenstein don't issue visas themselves and use nearby countries' systems.

Myth 3: "Greece is easy because they're desperate for tourism"

Reality: Greece has high approval rates (88%), but they still verify documents carefully. They want genuine tourists, not illegal workers disguised as tourists.

Myth 4: "If I get rejected by France, I can immediately apply to Greece"

Reality: All rejections are shared in the Schengen database. Greece will see your French rejection. You need to fix the rejection issues, not just try a different country.

Myth 5: "France/Germany/Italy reject more because they're stricter"

Reality: They receive MORE applications (including many weak ones), so their percentage looks lower. A well-prepared application succeeds in any country.

Myth 6: "I can apply for a Greece visa, then change my plans to go to France instead"

Reality: Dangerous. Border control may question you. If they find you lied about your main destination, you could be denied entry and banned from Schengen.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which country is absolutely the easiest to get a Schengen visa?

Based on pure approval rate statistics: Lithuania (97.2%), followed by Estonia (96.8%) and Finland (95.6%). However, "easiest" depends on your ability to legitimately justify visiting that country.

Can I apply to Lithuania even if I want to visit France?

Only if Lithuania is your main destination (most nights spent) OR your first entry point with equal nights. Otherwise, it's visa fraud and will lead to rejection/ban.

What if I'm visiting 5 countries equally?

Apply to your first entry point. Example: Flying into Greece, then visiting Italy, France, Spain, Germany for 2 days each? Apply to Greece.

Is Greece really easier than France or Germany?

Yes, statistically. Greece: 88% approval. France: 84.6%. Germany: 82.1%. But if you have strong documents, you'll likely get approved by any of them.

I got rejected by Germany. Should I try Greece now?

First, address the rejection reasons. Greece will see your German rejection in the system. Fix your application (better bank statements, clearer itinerary, etc.) before reapplying to any country.

Do all Schengen countries share visa information?

YES. All applications, approvals, and rejections are in the shared Visa Information System (VIS). You can't hide a rejection from one country when applying to another.

What's the minimum bank balance for easiest countries?

  • Estonia: €30/day (lowest!)
  • Lithuania: €40/day
  • Greece: €50/day
  • For 14-day trip: €420, €560, and €700 respectively

Which country processes visas fastest?

Estonia and Lithuania often process in 7-10 days. France and Italy can take 15-30 days during peak season. Processing Time Comparison →

Can I enter a different Schengen country than the one that issued my visa?

Yes, you can enter through any Schengen country. BUT your stated main destination should still be accurate. Border control might ask to see your itinerary.

Is it easier to get a visa if I've traveled to Schengen before?

YES! Previous compliance with Schengen visas significantly helps. First-time applicants face more scrutiny. That's why starting with an "easy" country like Greece for your first visa is smart.
