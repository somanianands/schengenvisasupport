This Cookie Policy explains how SchengenVisaSupport.com ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can:

  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Help you navigate between pages efficiently
  • Analyze how you use the website
  • Improve website performance
  • Deliver relevant advertising

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

Purpose: These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic features like page navigation and access to secure areas.

Examples:

  • Session cookies that remember your actions during a browsing session
  • Security cookies for authentication and fraud prevention

Can you refuse them? No. Essential cookies are strictly necessary for the website to work and cannot be disabled.

2.2 Analytics Cookies

Purpose: These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously.

What we use:

  • Google Analytics: Tracks user behavior, page views, time on site, bounce rate, and demographics
  • Collects information such as: pages visited, time spent on pages, links clicked, device type, browser, location (city/country level)

Data collected is anonymous and aggregated. We use this data to:

  • Improve website content and structure
  • Understand which pages are most popular
  • Identify technical issues
  • Optimize user experience

Can you refuse them? Yes. You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

2.3 Advertising Cookies

Purpose: These cookies are used to deliver advertisements that are relevant to you and your interests.

What we use:

  • Google AdSense: Displays ads on our website based on your browsing history and interests
  • Third-party ad networks: May also place cookies to show personalized ads

How advertising cookies work:

  • Track the websites you visit and ads you interact with
  • Build a profile of your interests
  • Deliver ads tailored to those interests
  • Limit the number of times you see the same ad
  • Measure ad effectiveness

Can you refuse them? Yes. You can opt out of personalized advertising:

  • Google Ads: Visit Google Ads Settings
  • Network Advertising Initiative: NAI Opt-Out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: DAA Opt-Out

2.4 Functionality Cookies

Purpose: These cookies remember your preferences and enhance your user experience.

Examples:

  • Remembering your language preference
  • Storing your font size or layout preferences
  • Keeping you logged in (if we offer user accounts)

Can you refuse them? Yes, but some features may not work as intended.

4. How Long Do Cookies Last?

Session Cookies

These are temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. They're used to maintain your session while you browse the website.

Persistent Cookies

These cookies remain on your device for a set period (ranging from days to years) or until you delete them manually. They remember your preferences across multiple visits.
Essential Cookies Session (deleted when browser closes)
Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics) 2 years
Advertising Cookies (AdSense) Up to 2 years

5. How to Manage and Delete Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. Here are your options:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Delete cookies after each browsing session
  • Receive notifications when cookies are set

How to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies

For detailed instructions: AboutCookies.org

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

For specific services, you can opt out directly:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Add-on
  • Google Ads: Google Ads Settings
  • Network Advertising Initiative: NAI Opt-Out Tool

5.3 Mobile Devices

On mobile devices (iOS, Android), you can manage cookies through browser settings or device privacy settings:

  • iOS: Settings → Safari → Block All Cookies
  • Android: Browser app → Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies, please note:

  • Some features of the website may not work properly
  • Your preferences won't be remembered
  • You may see less relevant advertisements
  • We won't be able to improve the website based on your usage patterns

Essential cookies are necessary for the website to function, so blocking all cookies may prevent you from using certain parts of the site.

7. Do Not Track (DNT) Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you don't want your online activity tracked.

Currently, there is no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals. Our website does not specifically respond to DNT signals, but you can still use the opt-out methods described above to limit tracking.
